# A01 — Real-World Applications of Computer Vision

**Date:** 01.21.2026

## Problem

Explore how computer vision is applied in a major real-world domain — demonstrating understanding of how CV systems perceive, process, and act on visual information in practical environments.

## Approach

Written essay analyzing self-driving autonomous vehicles as the primary case study. The paper examines how cameras and deep learning models work together with complementary sensors (LiDAR, radar, GPS) through sensor fusion to enable reliable perception in dynamic real-world conditions.

Topics covered: CNNs for road environment interpretation, sensor fusion combining visual and non-visual inputs, benefits of consistent and fatigue-free perception, and challenges including adverse weather, ethical decision-making in dangerous situations, and privacy concerns from continuous data collection.

## Results

Computer vision is the core perception layer in autonomous vehicles — without it, the system cannot identify lane markings, pedestrians, signs, or other vehicles. However, CV alone is insufficient; it requires fusion with radar and LiDAR to maintain reliability across varying lighting and weather. Ethical and regulatory frameworks are as important as the technical solutions themselves.

## Key Findings

The same CNN architecture used for basic image classification is the engine behind billion-dollar autonomous vehicle programs. The gap between research accuracy and real-world deployment is bridged by sensor redundancy, not model perfection.
